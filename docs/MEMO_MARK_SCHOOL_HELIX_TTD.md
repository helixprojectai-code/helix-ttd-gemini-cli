### 2. THE ARCHITECTURE: "THE GLASS SCHOOLHOUSE"
Instead of students "submitting a file" (which can be faked), the Helix Brick hosts the *environment* in which the work is done.

#### A. The Local Sovereign (The Brick)
- **The Hardware:** A high-compute node (Helix-TTD) physically sitting in the school server room.
- **The Data:** **Zero-Egress.** Student essays, logic, and patterns never leave the building. No data mining by Big Tech.
- **The Cost:** One-time CapEx (Purchase). No perpetual per-seat SaaS rent extracting value from the district.

#### B. The "Write-Only" Research Box (Proof of Thought)
You mentioned "write-only research boxes." In Helix architecture, we call this **"The Ledger of Thought."**

- **Mechanism:** Students don't just upload a PDF. They work in a Helix-hosted interface (local web app).
- **The Stream:** The system records the *construction* of the argument.
  - *Did this paragraph appear instantly (Paste)?* ‚Üí **Flag.**
  - *Did it evolve through edits, backspaces, and pauses (Human Thought)?* ‚Üí **Verify.**
- **Result:** The teacher sees not just the final essay, but a time-lapse of its creation. **Cheating becomes harder than doing the work.**

### 3. IDENTITY & EXTERNAL ACCESS

#### A. Key-Based Auth (The Student ID)
- Instead of a username/password (hackable), each student has a **Nostr Keypair** on their device.
- **Login:** The student scans a QR code. Cryptographic signature proves identity.
- **Remote Access:** Using the **Minder Protocol**, students connect from home via an encrypted tunnel *directly* to the School Brick. No third-party cloud sits in the middle.

#### B. Stylometric Fingerprinting (The "Voice" Check)
You asked about "difference in style."

- **The Baseline:** The Brick builds a local, private RLF model of each student's "Voice" (vocabulary, sentence structure, common errors) over the year.
- **The Check:** When a new assignment is submitted, the Brick compares it to the Baseline.
  - *Match:* 95% probability it's the student.
  - *Mismatch:* "This reads like ChatGPT-4o, not 10th Grade John." ‚Üí **Flag for Teacher Review.**
- **Crucial Difference:** The AI does not *grade* the paper (as Sir Ian fears). It acts as a **Minder**, flagging anomalies so the *human teacher* can intervene.
